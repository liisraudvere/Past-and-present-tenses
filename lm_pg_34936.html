Present and past tenses (2/3)

What tenses do we use to talk about the past?

The tenses we often use to talk about the past are:
  • past simple
    • used for completed actions in the past
  • past continuous
    • used for unfinished actions in progress at a certain time in the past
If you feel confident enough using these tenses, you can scroll down to the review and do the practice exercises.
If you need more information and help, please click on the panels below to find out more about the form and usage of each tense.
Past simple
When do we use the past simple tense?

We use the past simple 
  • to talk about finished events that took place at a finished time
  • with specific time words talking about a finished time (yesterday, last week/month/year/etc., when I was young)

How do we form the past simple tense?

We need the -ed ending for regular verbs or the 2nd form of irregular verbs.

Past continuous
How and when do we use the past continuous tense?

We use the past continuous to 
  • talk about events in progress at a specific time in the past (At 7 pm I was eating dinner = I was in the middle of the process of eating)
  • talk about interrupted and unfinished events in the past (Maj Jones was writing an OPORD when the phone rang = We have no information about whether he finished writing after that) 
  • describe the background or scene of a story (The sun was shining, the birds were singing)
  • show that two events were taking place at the same time (Tom was polishing his boots while the CO was looking for him.)

How do I know if I need to use the past simple or continuous?

The main difference between the simple and continuous tenses is the aspect of progression or continuity - the continuous tense always shows that something was or is in progress at a specific time.

With the past tenses, the easiest solution is to think about whether the activity is finished.
